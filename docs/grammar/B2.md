# B2 Grammar Plan (Speaking First)

## 1. Advanced Tense Precision
- Present Perfect Continuous
- Past Perfect and Past Perfect Continuous
- Future Perfect / Future Perfect Continuous (functional use)
- Advanced tense sequencing in narratives

## 2. Passive and Causative Systems
- Passive voice across key tenses
- Passive with modals (`must be done`)
- Causative refinement (`have/get something done`)
- Impersonal passive (`It is said that...`)

## 3. Conditionals and Hypotheticals
- Mixed conditionals
- Third Conditional
- `wish/if only` with past forms
- `would rather` / `would prefer`

## 4. Modal Nuance
- Past modals: `might have`, `must have`, `can't have`
- Obligation spectrum: `must`, `have to`, `need to`, `be supposed to`
- Certainty spectrum in argumentation

## 5. Complex Clauses and Cohesion
- Non-defining relative clauses
- Participle clauses (intro use in speech)
- Concession/contrast: `even though`, `whereas`, `despite`
- Purpose/result clauses: `so that`, `in order to`, `such...that`

## 6. Reported Speech and Framing
- Reported speech full system (statements, questions, commands)
- Reporting verbs: `admit, suggest, deny, recommend`
- Hedging structures: `It seems`, `It appears`, `I would argue`

## 7. Discourse and Interaction Management
- Turn-taking grammar: `If I may add...`
- Reformulation: `In other words...`
- Diplomacy grammar: `I see your point, but...`

## 8. Speaking Outcomes (B2)
- Defend position with layered arguments
- Compare alternatives with nuance
- Handle disagreement and negotiation
- Maintain 12-15 minute discussion
